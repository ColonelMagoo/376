Waluigi's debut came in Mario Tennis on the N64 because Wario was planned to be in the game but was the only character without a duos partner. It was because of this that they created a new character. Like Wario, he is considered to be a more exagerated form of his counterpart Luigi.

It is because of this appearence that he is often depicted with a Tennis Racket in his hand.

It was at Mario Party 3, where he was featured as the main antagonist, that he would become a reaccurring character
